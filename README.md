# Smart Biomass Reduction System (SBRS) 🌍🔋

An open-source, ultra-compact organic waste processor designed to eliminate leachate and methane emissions at the source. By integrating mechanical chopping, continuous screw-pressing, dynamic decanter centrifugation, and PTC thermal convection into a single, dual-bearing shaft, the SBRS reduces wet waste volume by up to 80% within a 5-minute cycle.

---

📜 Technical Blueprint & "Recipe" for the Smart Biomass Reduction System (SBRS)Designed by: Hossein Sadeghi NejadMission: Radical reduction of municipal waste mass, elimination of groundwater toxic leachate, and local energy optimization.🛠️ Step-by-Step Mechanical "Recipe" (The Process Workflow)To process any organic waste—from standard kitchen scraps to high-moisture watermelon rinds—the SBRS executes a strict 5-minute linear workflow via its unified 30cm horizontal shaft:Step 1: The Blunt Intake & Axial TrappingAction: Waste is directly introduced into the Direct-Feed Mechanical Intake without any vertical funnels.The Physics: The intake relies on gravity and a horizontal offset. The initial segment of the shaft features jagged, shark-tooth flights. These teeth grip the bulky waste and push it forcefully against fixed internal axial ribs on the cylinder wall, cleanly slicing it into manageable $2\text{--}3\text{ cm}$ cubes.Step 2: Continuous Progressive CompactionAction: The cubed material is immediately pushed into the Tapered Screw Press zone.The Physics: As the shaft diameter expands and the flight pitch shrinks, the material faces immense mechanical pressure. This physical compression ruptures the cellular walls of the biomass, instantly squeezing out more than 50% of the bound internal liquid.Step 3: High-Speed Centrifugal DecantingAction: The heavy sludge is dynamically separated into dry solids and clear liquid.The Physics: The material lands inside a dual-rotation chamber. The inner scroll keeps transporting the sludge at ~80 RPM, while the outer wedge-wire screen cylinder rotates rapidly at ~1800 RPM. Centrifugal force flings the water outward through 0.3mm micro-slots into the filtrate tray, leaving a dewatered, non-sticky biomass "cake" behind.Step 4: Vortex Thermal ConvectionAction: The biomass cake enters the final 10cm dehydration tunnel.The Physics: A blower fan forces air past a self-regulating ceramic PTC heating element, blasting a 120°C heat vortex through the chamber. The material is tumbled at a low-pressure pitch, evaporating all remaining surface moisture into vapor, which is vented out through an active carbon filter.Step 5: Pure Biomass DischargeAction: The cycle completes as the system drops the output into the collection bin.The Physics: The fully processed, sterile, and bone-dry biomass falls from the discharge end. It resembles damp sawdust but is completely odorless and stable, preventing any bacteria from ever breeding.🔬 Engineering Disasters Warded Off: Problems & Solutions LogTrue sustainability is not just about a pretty idea; it is about keeping the machine from breaking under pressure. Here is how we solved the major engineering bottlenecks of this project:🚨 Problem 1: The In-feed Clogging & Material Free-SpinningThe Disaster: When soft skins (like banana or orange peels) hit a rotating screw, they tend to just spin in circles with the blade instead of moving forward, causing a massive clog at the mouth.The Solution: We designed Counter-Rotational Grooves on the inner casing and a Gravity-Assisted Offset Feed. The offset drops the waste perfectly onto the downward-moving flight, while the fixed grooves act as brakes against the waste. Because the waste cannot rotate, the laws of physics force it to slide axially forward.🚨 Problem 2: Screen Clogging and High-Maintenance ScrapersThe Disaster: Pressing organic waste creates a sticky, glue-like paste that easily plugs up filtration holes. Traditional rubber or plastic scrapers wear out within weeks and cause heavy motor friction.The Solution: We eliminated consumables entirely by using a Differential Speed Decanter Centrifuge. The inner scroll and outer slotted screen spin in the same direction but at slightly different speeds. This speed delta allows the inner metal flights to act as a permanent, non-contacting mechanical wiper that continuously shaves off the biomass cake without any metal-to-metal friction or parts that wear out.🚨 Problem 3: Shaft Deflection & Structural Deflection Under Heavy PressingThe Disaster: Crushing dense materials requires immense torque. Using a single-ended (cantilever) shaft means the rod will bend like a bow, instantly ruining the 0.3mm tight tolerances of the centrifuge.The Solution: A Rigid Dual-Bearing Configuration. The drive end is locked directly into a high-torque worm-gear DC motor gearbox, while the terminal discharge end is securely anchored inside a heat-and-water-resistant wall-mounted Flange Bearing. Deflection is kept strictly below $<0.1\text{mm}$.🚨 Problem 4: Thermal Back-Creep and Motor MeltingThe Disaster: Steel is an excellent conductor of heat. The 120°C heat from the drying phase will travel backward down the unified shaft, destroying the centrifuge, melting the rubber seals, and frying the main motor's grease.The Solution: We introduced a Ceramic Thermal-Break Coupling right between the centrifuge zone and the heating zone. Movement passes through seamlessly, but heat conduction stops dead in its tracks. Furthermore, we shifted from direct conduction heating to Convective PTC Airflow, keeping the main structural shaft isolated from dangerous heat.🌍 Conclusion: Engineering a Greener Future (The UWC Vision)The SBRS is not just a smart appliance; it is a compact solution to a massive global ecological problem. By tackling wet domestic waste at its exact point of generation, this system introduces three key shifts in environmental management:Environmental Stewardship: By removing the moisture instantly, we completely halt the generation of municipal landfill leachate. This saves vulnerable underground freshwater tables from heavy chemical and acidic poisoning worldwide.Resource Optimization: Slashed mass means optimized logistics. Lowering urban trash weight by up to 80% means an immediate reduction in the frequency of garbage truck routes, drastically lowering diesel consumption and localized greenhouse emissions.Closing the Bioloop: Instead of throwing away precious organic matter to rot under anaerobic conditions (which releases toxic methane), the SBRS delivers a sterile, pH-balanced, dry biomass that is ready to be instantly converted into high-grade agricultural compost.Final Pitch: Innovation is not about making things more complex; it is about mastering the laws of mechanics to make sustainability simple, durable, and decentralized. The SBRS proves that with the right engineering, we can stop managing waste crises and start processing raw environmental resources right inside our homes.
